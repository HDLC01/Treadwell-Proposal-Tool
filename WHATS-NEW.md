# Treadwell Proposal Generator — What's New

*A quick, plain-English rundown of the estimate/proposal tool and the latest improvements. Written for the team — no tech background needed.*

**Where it lives:** https://proposals.wetreadwell.com (works on any computer or phone, no login needed)

---

## What this tool does, in one sentence

You fill in a short form about a job, and it spits out a finished **estimate spreadsheet** and a **Word proposal** — priced correctly — and files them in Dropbox for you.

## How you use it (4 quick screens)

1. **Intake** — basic job info: project name, address, due date, square footage, how many floor systems.
2. **Estimate Review** — pick the floor system(s); the tool prices everything for you. Toggle bulk discount, pick the county for remodel tax, add any extra materials.
3. **Proposal Review** — check the proposal wording and the price.
4. **Done** — hit **Generate**. It creates the estimate + proposal and saves them to Dropbox. You get download links and the folder.

---

## What's new and better

**✅ It does the pricing for you — and it's exact.**
The tool now calculates the bid itself straight from Kyle's 5.7 estimate numbers. The price it shows matches the estimate sheet **to the dollar**. No more "#NAME?" errors or guessing.

**✅ Multiple floor systems in one job.**
Got an epoxy area and a different system in another area? Enter as many systems as the job needs — it prices each one and adds them up.

**✅ Bulk discount + county remodel tax built in.**
- Flip the **Bulk discount** switch and the right materials drop to bulk pricing automatically.
- Search your **county** and it uses the correct Kansas remodel-tax rate (state 6.5% + your county's share) — not a flat guess.

**✅ Add your own materials ("extras").**
Need to add something like *Super Stick*, *Floor Graphic*, or any one-off material? There's an **Extra materials** box — type the item, quantity, and price, and it flows into the total. Add as many as you need.

**✅ One price, everywhere.**
The number you see on the estimate screen, the proposal screen, the final screen, and inside the saved files are now **all the same**. (We fixed an old glitch where the final screen could show $0 or a wrong number.)

**✅ Tax is handled right.**
The price already includes sales tax and Kansas remodel tax. The proposal correctly reads **"Kansas Remodel Tax"** and the line items add up properly.

**✅ Files save automatically — with the team's naming.**
Generated files land in Dropbox under
**Estimating → $Commercial Sales Estimates → \*Hanz → Hanz Estimate Automation**,
in a project folder named by due date, e.g. `26.09.15 Acme Plant !`. Inside you'll find:
- `$estimate sheet - <Project>.xlsx`
- `MM.DD TREADWELL EPOXY PROPOSAL - New Direct.docx`

**✅ It's much faster.**
The estimate screen used to take ~15 seconds to open — now it's about 2.

## Good to know

- **You're always in control.** The tool drafts; you review every screen before anything is generated. Nothing is ever sent to a customer automatically — you download and send it yourself.
- **You can override anything.** Every priced cell can be edited by hand if a job has a special case.
- **Your work is saved as you go.** If you close the tab or switch devices, your in-progress job is still there.
